Scotland

About the Country

Land of Lochs & Legends

Scotland is a country in Europe and is part of the island of Great Britain (Europe's Largest Island) alongside England, Wales, and Northern Ireland. This lush beautiful country is bursting with green spaces, lush forests, towering mountains and vast lochs (the Scottish word for lakes!).

This country can be roughly divided into three areas ‚Äî Lowlands, Highlands and Islands. The Lowlands are known for their fertile farmland and thick woodlands, the Highlands for their towering mountains, sweeping moorland and deep lochs, and the Islands for their compact wild landscapes, beautiful beaches and far-reaching sea views.

Places to Visit in Scotland

01
Edinburgh Castle

Perched atop Castle Rock, Edinburgh Castle is a historic fortress that offers panoramic views of the city and houses Scotland's Crown Jewels. It has played a key role in Scottish history for centuries.

02
Kelvingrove Art Museum

Located in Glasgow, Kelvingrove Art Museum is home to an extensive collection of art, artifacts, and natural history exhibits. Its impressive architecture and free admission make it a popular cultural destination.

03
Culzean Castle

Overlooking the Firth of Clyde, Culzean Castle is an elegant 18th-century mansion known for its grand interiors and scenic cliffside gardens. A popular spot for exploring Scottish history and nature.

04
Portree, Isle of Skye

Portree is a picturesque harbor town on the Isle of Skye, famous for its colorful buildings and dramatic coastal landscapes. A gateway for exploring the island's natural beauty and hiking trails.

05
The Calanais Standing Stones

An ancient stone circle on the Isle of Lewis, dating back over 5,000 years. These mysterious megaliths are steeped in legend and offer insight into prehistoric life.

06
The Jacobite Steam Train

A historic train travelling through the scenic West Highland Line, famously passing over the Glenfinnan Viaduct ‚Äî known for its appearance in the Harry Potter films.
